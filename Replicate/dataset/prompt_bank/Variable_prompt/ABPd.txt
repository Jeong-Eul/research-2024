Arterial Blood Pressure Diastolic (ABPd) is the measurement of the pressure in the arteries when the heart is relaxed and filling with blood. In other words, it represents the pressure in the blood vessels when the heart stops pumping and is at rest. Typically, a normal range is known to be between 60 mmHg and 80 mmHg. Low diastolic blood pressure indicates that the pressure within the blood vessels is very low.
